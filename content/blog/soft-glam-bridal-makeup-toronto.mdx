## What soft glam bridal makeup actually means

Soft glam sits between "everyday natural" and "full editorial." The goal is a luminous, polished finish — not heavy, not bare. Think: skin that looks like great skin, not like it's been painted. Eyes that are defined and lifted without being overwhelming. A lip that completes the look without competing with your outfit or jewellery.

For South Asian brides specifically, soft glam photographs well under the full spectrum of lighting a Toronto wedding day throws at you. From morning ceremony light at a gurdwara in Scarborough to the tungsten warmth of a banquet hall in North York, to the overexposed flash of your reception photographer — this style holds up across all of it.

### The skin is the foundation (literally)

The biggest mistake I see with bridal makeup that doesn't last is a poorly prepped base. No matter how premium the products are, if your skin isn't moisturised, primed correctly, and properly set, the makeup will slide or crease within a few hours. For a full South Asian wedding day in Toronto — ceremony, reception, baraat, and beyond — I use a layered approach: a hydrating primer, a lightweight HD foundation matched to your undertone, strategic concealing, and a finishing mist that locks everything in place.

The brands in my kit — DIOR, Charlotte Tilbury, and Chanel — are there because they were built for high-definition photography and long wear. These aren't aspirational choices; they're professional tools.

## Soft glam across South Asian wedding traditions

One reason I love working with Toronto brides is how much variety there is. A Punjabi wedding has a different energy than a Hindu ceremony, which feels different again from a Muslim nikkah. Each tradition has its own aesthetic code, and soft glam is flexible enough to adapt to all of them.

For a **mehndi or sangeet**, I'll lean into warm terracotta tones, a glossier lip, and softer eyes — the look should feel festive and celebratory, not bridal-formal. For the **wedding ceremony itself**, the look is elevated: more defined eyes, a deeper or more structured lip, the kind of finish that reads beautifully from 10 feet away in a mandap or under chuppah flowers. For a **reception** in the evening, there's often room for a touch more drama — a deeper liner flick, a richer cheek.

The conversation about which look lands where is part of every consultation I have with Toronto brides. I've worked at venues across the city — from Scarborough and North York to Etobicoke and downtown Toronto — and the venue matters too. Bright outdoor light calls for a slightly different approach than the warm, low light of an elegant indoor hall.

## How to prepare for your appointment

A few things that make a real difference on the day:

**Skincare in the weeks before.** Consistent moisturiser and SPF are more valuable than any last-minute facial. If you're getting professional treatments, stop anything exfoliating at least 10 days before your wedding.

**Arrive with a clean face.** No makeup, no heavy moisturiser on the morning of your appointment. Product residue affects how the base adheres and how long it lasts.

**Wash your hair without conditioner the night before** if you're having your hair styled. Conditioner softens the hair and reduces hold — it makes styling harder and shortens how long it lasts.

**Bring your outfit and jewellery to the trial.** This is the only way to make sure the makeup makes sense in context.
